Prayer Tower

History of The Prayer Tower

Late 1990, the then Minister in charge of the KNUST Presbyterian Church, Rev. G. K. Acheampong and the Session found it necessary to form the KNUST Prayer Tower team.

Click to read more...

The Purpose

  • To intercede for the church.
  • To counsel and pray for members of the congregation who need spiritual assistance.

Members of the Team

The Session handpicked and appointed the following to form the nucleus team.

  • Mr. Seth Kwabena Adu Baah
  • Mr. Solomon Osei
  • Mr. Moses Opoku
  • Mr. Nii Nai Mensah (Jnr.)
  • Mr. Sowah Mensah
  • Mr. Samuel Adu-Poku
  • Mr. Kwaku Gyasi Acheampong (Now Rev. Minister)
  • Mr. Auckhinleck Kwame Adow
  • Mrs. Rose Ohene Adjei
  • Mrs. Lydia Atakora
  • Miss Regina Dwamena

All members gladly accepted to be part of the team. The team elected Mr Seth Kwabena Adu Baah to be the leader of the team. The decision was sent to the Session for approval and was accepted.

Activities of The Team

The team was made up of

  • Counseling Group led by Mr. Seth Kwabena Adu Baah
  • Deliverance group led by Mr. Nii Nai Mensah.(Jnr.)

Bible studies, Fasting and prayers, retreats, church and outreach revivals were also part of the activities. Team-up programmes were also organised with the Anwomaso Presbyterian Prayer Tower team.

New Members

In 1992 the team co-opted some new members to assist. They were:

  • Mr. Seth Asante
  • Mr. Theophilus Nai Mensah
  • Mr. Ebenezer Henaku Twum (Now Rev. Minister)
  • Mr. Charles Ofosu.

In 1995 due to transfer and further studies of some members of the team, the number of the members was reduced to four. The Lord added new members from the Bible Study and Prayer Group (BSPG). They were:

  • Mrs. Gladys Asibey
  • Mr. Emmanuel Obour (Now Rev. Minister)
  • Miss Cecilia Ohemeng
  • Miss Comfort Nyarko
  • Miss Gladys Mante (Now Catechist)

In 1999 new members were recruited from the Young Peoples Guild (YPG). They were:

  • Mr. Godfred Adjekum
  • Mr. Gabriel Acheampong
  • Mr. George Ansong (Now Presbyter)
  • Mr. Ebenezer Kumi
  • Miss Veronica Abrokwa
  • Miss Naomi Akrofi

Brigade

History of The Brigade

The idea of establishing a Brigade in the Church was proposed by Cat. Mrs. Gloria Okine and Cat. Joseph W. Fynn in 1984. With the encouragement from the late Rt. Rev. Daniel Agyei Koranten, then University of Science and Technology, Kumasi Protestant Chaplain and Minister in charge of the Church (UST Protestant Vernacular Church) permission was sought from the Church leaders and the Executive Committee. In the same year (1984) two Basic training courses were organized for the following church members who voluntarily offered themselves to start the Brigade movement in the Church.

Pioneers of the Brigade

  • Cat. Joseph William Fynn
  • Cat. Mrs. Gloria Okine
  • Mr. Hayford Johnson Akwasi Okumkah
  • Late Rev. Eric Ahmed Adams
  • Mr. Joshua Asamani
  • Mr. Ebenezer Anshong Labi
  • Mr. William Addo
  • Mrs. Grace Osei Boateng
  • Mrs. Theresa Osei Bonsu
  • Mrs. Regina Okine
  • Miss Mary Korantemaa Opoku
  • Mrs. Victoria Wilhelmina Bansah
  • Miss Margaret Osei Bonsu.

The initial recruitment drive brought together over one hundred and twenty (120) boys and girls from the church. The first enrolment service was held on 7th July 1985 with five (5) Boys’ Officers, twenty (20) Boys made up of thirteen (13) Company Section Boys and . Seven (7) Girls’ Officers, thirty-three (33) Girls made up of five (5) Brigaders, fifteen (15) Senior Girls, eight (8) Junior Section and five (5) Explorers.
